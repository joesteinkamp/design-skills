# Common Design Spectrums

Reference library of recurring design tensions. Use as a starting point — always prioritize challenge-specific spectrums over generic ones. Not all spectrums apply to every challenge; select only those that represent consequential decisions.

## Interaction Model

- **Guided ←→ Exploratory:** Step-by-step wizard vs. open-ended sandbox.
- **Simple ←→ Powerful:** Fewer options with less learning curve vs. full control with complexity.
- **Automated ←→ Manual:** System decides for the user vs. user retains full control.
- **Immediate ←→ Deliberate:** Quick actions with less precision vs. considered actions with more precision.
- **Linear ←→ Non-linear:** Fixed sequence of steps vs. flexible, user-directed navigation.

## Information Density

- **Dense ←→ Spacious:** Maximum information per screen vs. focused, progressive disclosure.
- **Overview-first ←→ Detail-first:** Start with the big picture vs. start with a specific item.
- **Exhaustive ←→ Curated:** Show everything available vs. show the most relevant subset.
- **Flat ←→ Layered:** All information on one level vs. nested progressive disclosure.

## Personalization & Defaults

- **Opinionated ←→ Neutral:** Strong defaults that push a direction vs. equal presentation of all options.
- **Personalized ←→ Universal:** Adapted to individual behavior vs. same experience for everyone.
- **Rigid ←→ Flexible:** Consistent, constrained experience vs. user-customizable experience.

## Tone & Communication

- **Conversational ←→ Transactional:** Warm, human language vs. efficient, minimal interface copy.
- **Educational ←→ Assumed-knowledge:** Explain concepts as you go vs. assume expertise.
- **Reassuring ←→ Efficient:** Confirm and explain each step vs. minimize friction and steps.
- **Playful ←→ Serious:** Personality-driven, delightful interactions vs. professional, no-nonsense.

## Trust & Safety

- **Permissive ←→ Protective:** Let users take risks freely vs. guard against mistakes.
- **Transparent ←→ Abstracted:** Show how things work under the hood vs. hide complexity.
- **Reversible ←→ Committed:** Easy undo, everything is a draft vs. deliberate, consequential actions.

## Scope & Focus

- **Specialist ←→ Generalist:** Do one thing exceptionally vs. cover many use cases adequately.
- **Integrated ←→ Standalone:** Deep connections to ecosystem vs. self-contained experience.
- **Proactive ←→ Reactive:** System initiates and suggests vs. system waits for user input.

## Visual & Layout

- **Minimal ←→ Rich:** Stripped-back, content-focused vs. visually expressive with illustration, color, imagery.
- **Structured ←→ Organic:** Grid-based, predictable layouts vs. flowing, content-adaptive layouts.
- **Uniform ←→ Differentiated:** Consistent treatment for all items vs. visual hierarchy that distinguishes importance.

## Usage Notes

- Choose 5-8 spectrums per design challenge. More than 8 becomes noise.
- Discard any spectrum where the right answer is obvious for the context.
- Always define poles in the context of the specific challenge — don't use these labels verbatim if they don't fit.
- The best spectrums are challenge-specific ones not on this list.
