# AI-Native Workshop Instructions (Reference)

Use this reference when the user asks for AI-Native workshop prompts or asks to mirror the previous AI-Native workshop setup.

## Canonical Workshop Agenda

- Part 1: Context Setting (30 min)
- Part 2: React, Riff & Add (25 min)
- Part 3: Solutions & Signals (15 min)
- Part 4: Open Discussion (40 min)
- Wrap-Up (5 min)

Alternative sequence used in a close variant:
- Part 1: Why This Matters (15 min)
- Part 2: What's Going on Around the Org (15 min)
- Part 3: What This Looks Like for Design (15 min)
- Part 4: How Do We Get There (40 min)

## Core Framing Content

### Vision

Heading:
- `What "AI-Native" Means for Us`

Anchor line:
- `Use AI every day, in everything we do.`

Goal statements used in the workshop:
- Raise craft quality (divergence, state coverage, responsive detail).
- Ship prototypes, not static screens.
- Build designer comfort with code and shipping.
- Increase experimentation beyond PRDs.
- Strengthen PM-like problem framing.
- Back design decisions with user insights.
- Improve judgment on when to use which tools.

### Current Problems

Heading:
- `Barriers to Becoming AI-Native`

Theme summary:
- Busy schedules reduce learning bandwidth.
- Incentives to upskill are unclear.
- Engagement is uneven across the team.
- Productivity gains are not well measured.
- Hiring for this skill profile is difficult.
- Tooling landscape changes too quickly.
- Tool-choice guidance is unclear.
- Curiosity vs capacity remains unresolved.

### Use Cases

Heading:
- `Where AI Fits Into Our Work`

Use cases:
- Rapid prototyping.
- Visual exploration.
- Content and microcopy generation.
- Design-to-code translation.
- User research synthesis.
- Responsive adaptation.
- QA and edge-case discovery.

## Exercise Prompts

### Exercise 1: React, Riff & Add

Working prompt:
- `Looking at the goals and problems - what's missing? What's wrong? What would you change? Drop stickies anywhere.`

Flow:
- Sticky Note Dump (10 min)
- Talk It Out (15 min)

Board zones:
- `GOALS - Your Reactions`
- `PROBLEMS - Your Reactions`

### Exercise 2: Solutions & Success Signals

Working prompt:
- `How do we enable our team? How do we solve our problems? How do we track we're on the right track?`

Sub-prompts:
- `What specific actions could we take? Think training, tools, process, culture - anything goes.`
- `In 90 days, what would tell us we're making real progress? Think observable behaviors, not dashboards.`

Flow:
- Brainstorm Solutions (8 min)
- Success Signals (7 min)

Board zones:
- `SOLUTIONS`
- `SUCCESS SIGNALS`

### Exercise 2 Variant: Requirements & Solutions

Working prompt:
- `What does our team need? What specific actions could we take to get there?`

Sub-prompts:
- `What does success look like? What must be true to achieve this? Think observable behaviors, outcomes, and goals.`
- `What specific actions could we take? Think training, tools, process, culture - anything goes.`

Flow:
- Define Requirements (8 min)
- Brainstorm Solutions (7 min)

Board zones:
- `REQUIREMENTS`
- `SOLUTIONS`

### Exercise 3: Open Discussion - Now What?

Working prompt:
- `Turn alignment into action. Figure out what we do next, what we need, and what we're willing to change.`

Discussion prompts:
- `What can we do tomorrow to start moving on this?`
- `What do we need to be successful? (tools, time, support, permission)`
- `Could we get off Figma? Should we?`
- `What specific actions would solve the problems we identified?`
- `In 90 days, what would tell us we're making real progress?`
- `Who owns what? What's the first thing each of us commits to in the next two weeks?`

Board zones:
- `COMMITMENTS & OWNERS`
- `IMMEDIATE NEXT STEPS`
- `FUTURE NEXT STEPS`

## Wrap-Up Capture

Capture these in final synthesis:
- Recap: key decisions
- Owners: who does what
- Next steps: immediate actions
