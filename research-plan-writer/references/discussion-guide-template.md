# Discussion Guide Template

Reference for writing effective semi-structured interview and discussion guides.

## Guide Structure

### 1. Introduction (5 min)
- Introduce yourself and your role.
- Explain the purpose: "We're learning about [topic] to improve [product/experience]."
- Set expectations: session length, format, recording.
- Consent: confirm recording permission and explain how data will be used.
- Ground rules:
  - No wrong answers.
  - We want honest feedback—critical feedback is the most helpful.
  - You can skip any question or stop at any time.
  - We're researching the experience, not testing you.

### 2. Warm-Up (5 min)
- Build rapport with easy, open-ended questions.
- Establish context about the participant's background.
- Example: "Tell me a little about your role and how you typically [relevant activity]."

### 3. Core Questions (30-40 min)
- Group by theme (3-4 themes).
- Start each theme broad, then narrow.
- 2-3 primary questions per theme, each with 2-3 probes.

### 4. Cool-Down (5 min)
- Open the floor: "Anything else you want to share?"
- Meta question: "What should I have asked that I didn't?"
- Thank the participant.

## Question Writing Rules

### Do
- Ask open-ended questions: "Tell me about...", "Walk me through...", "Describe..."
- Ask about specific past behavior: "Tell me about the last time you..."
- Use the participant's language, not product jargon.
- Sequence from broad to specific within each theme.
- Include probes for going deeper.

### Don't
- Ask leading questions: "Don't you think X is confusing?" → "How was your experience with X?"
- Ask hypothetical questions: "Would you use X?" → "Tell me about a time you needed X."
- Ask double-barreled questions: "How do you feel about the price and quality?" → Split into two.
- Ask yes/no questions as primary questions (ok as follow-ups).
- Use jargon or internal terminology.

## Probe Types

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Clarification | Understand what they said | "Can you say more about that?" |
| Example | Get specific instances | "Can you give me an example?" |
| Sequence | Understand process/order | "Walk me through what happened next." |
| Emotional | Understand feelings | "How did that make you feel?" |
| Contrast | Understand comparisons | "How does that compare to [alternative]?" |
| Silence | Let them fill the gap | (Pause 5-7 seconds) |

## Discussion Guide Quality Checklist

- [ ] Introduction explains purpose without biasing responses
- [ ] Consent and recording are addressed
- [ ] Questions progress from broad to specific
- [ ] No leading, hypothetical, or double-barreled questions
- [ ] Each theme has primary questions and probes
- [ ] Cool-down includes an open-ended catch-all
- [ ] Guide fits within the session time budget
- [ ] Language matches the participant's vocabulary, not internal jargon
- [ ] Key research questions are covered by at least 2 discussion questions
